Extraocular muscles control the movements of the eyeball and the superior eyelid. There are eight extraocular muscles in each eye. They are:

A. Orbicularis Oculi

B. Levator palpebrae superioris

C. Two oblique muscles

1. Superior Oblique Muscle

2. Inferior Oblique Muscle

D. Four recti muscles

1. Superior Rectus Muscle

2. Inferior Rectus Muscle

3. Medial Rectus Muscle

4. Lateral Rectus Muscle

A. ORBICULARIS OCULI ( SPHINCTER MUSCLE AROUND THE EYE)

Orbicularis Oculi together with the corrugator supercilii and levator palpebrae superioris forms the orbital group of muscles. There are three (3) parts of Orbicularis Oculi. They are:

1. The orbital part

2. The palpebrae part

3. The lacrimal part

1. THE ORBITAL PART

The orbital part surround the orbit

ORIGIN

The orbital part originate from three points. They are:

1. The frontal bone

2. Anterior lacrimal crest

3. Frontal process of the maxilla

INSERTION

No bony insertion but attach to the skin of the lateral part

ACTION

Forceful closure of the eye in the presence of a threat. E.g. High intensity of light, foreign bodies etc.

NOTE; The orbital part exhibit forceful closure because it has no bony insertion but instead skin, hence, closure causes the entire skin to droop forcefully.

2. THE PALPEBRAE PART

The palpebrae surrounds the upper and lower eyelids.

ORIGIN

Medial palpebrae ligament

INSERTIION

Lateral palpebrae raphe which lies in front of the lateral palpebrae ligament.

ACTION

1. Gently closure of eye - This happens during blinking or sleeping

2. Reflex closure - This action moisturizes the cornea and also causes the release of tear film.

3. THE LACRIMAL PART

ORIGIN

Posterior lacrimal crest and part of the wall of lacrimal sac.

INSERTION

Fuse with palpebrae part of the muscle.

ACTION

Its function is to assist in drainage of tears.

NERVE SUPPLY

Branches of the facial nerve. Mainly:

1. The upper and lower zygomatic branches

2. The temporal branch

CLINICAL SIGNIFICANCE OF ORBICULARIS OCULI

Injury to facial nerve results in a condition called Bell’s palsy.

CHARACTERISTICS OF BELL PALSY

1. Inability furrow brow

2. Drooping of eyelid and inability to close eye

3. No muscle tone in cheek

4. Drooping mouth and inability to smile or pucker lip.

B. LEVATOR PALPEBRAE SUPERIORIS

The name of this muscles gives a lot of description about it. Palpebrae means eyelid, superioris means presence in the upper eyelid and levator signifies its action to elevate the upper eyelid.

POSITION

Between the eyeball and orbit.

ORIGIN

Sphenoid bone

INSERTION

Tarsal plate, upper eyelid.

INNERVATION

Innerve by the superior division of the oculomotor nerve (Cranial nerve III)

CLINICAL SIGNIFICANCE

Ptosis

C. THE TWO OBLIQUE MUSCLES

As their name implies, these muscles approaches the eye at an angle.

C1 – Superior Oblique Muscle

ORIGIN

Originate from the body of sphenoid through the trochlear.

INSERTION

Attaches to the sclera

ACTION

1. The main of the superior oblique muscle is depression, which is at best when the eye is adducted.

The subservient actions includes:

2. Abduction

3. Medial rotation(intorsion)

NOTE: The subservient actions increases as the eye is abducted.

NERVE SUPPLY

It is innerve by trochlear nerve (Cranial nerve IV)

C2 – Inferior Oblique Muscle

ORIGIN

Originates from the medial side of the orbital floor

INSERTION

Attaches to the sclera and a convex and upward manner.

ACTIONS

1. The main action is elevation, which is at its maximum in the adducted position.

The subservient actions includes:

2. Adduction

3. Lateral rotation(extorsion)

NOTE: The subservient actions increases as the eye is abducted.

NERVE SUPPLY

It is innerve by the inferior division of the oculomotor nerve (Cranial nerve III)

D. THE FOUR RECTUS MUSCLES

ORIGIN

The four rectus muscles(refer to figure B) take origin in part from the bones at the apex of the orbit, but also from the tendinous annulus.

INSERTION

They course anteriorly to insert into the sclera anterior to the equator pf the globe. Generally considered to be tendinous at the site of insertion, the medial and rectus muscles in human may contain myofibres that extend directly to the sclera.

1. ACTIONS OF SUPERIOR RECTUS MUSCLE

i. Elevation

ii. Adduction

iii. Medial rotation(intorsion)

NB: Elevation is its main function

NERVE SUPPLY

It is innerved by the oculomotor nerve (Cranial nerve III)

2. ACTIONS OF INFERIOR OBLIQUE MUSCLE

i. Depression

ii. Adduction

iii. Lateral rotation(extorsion)

NERVE SUPPLY

It is innerved by the oculomotor nerve (Cranial nerve III)

3. ACTION OF MEDIAL RECTUS MUSCLE

- Adds to abduction

NERVE SUPPLY

It is innerved by the oculomotor nerve (Cranial nerve III)

4. ACTION OF LATERAL NERVE

- Adds to abduction

NERVE SUPPLY

Abducens nerve (Cranial nerve VI)

CLINICAL SIGNIFICANCE

1. STRABISMUS

Misalignment of the eyes such that disparate images reach corresponding parts of each retina, disrupting binocular vision.

2. NYSTAGMUS

Nystagmus is defined as a bilateral, involuntary and conjugate oscillation of the eyes, and is primarily a problem of the oculomotor control with the brain.
